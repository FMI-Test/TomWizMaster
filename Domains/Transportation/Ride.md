## Core Premise

> **"The ride is the product. The driver is the concierge."**

A ride is not a commodity. It is a hospitality experience delivered inside a moving vehicle. Every touchpoint — from booking to drop-off — is a product moment.

## The Ride Experience — End to End

### 1. Pre-Ride

- Booking confirmation with driver profile (name, vehicle, ETA)
- Pre-trip communication: "I'm 4 minutes away. Your Escalade IQ is black, plate [XXX]."
- Vehicle presented clean, fully charged, climate pre-set

### 2. Arrival

- Driver exits vehicle and opens door (Black/Lux standard)
- Luggage loaded by driver without being asked
- Greeting: brief, professional, no over-talk
- Confirmation of destination and any route preferences

### 3. In-Ride

- Silence as the default — driver speaks when spoken to
- Ambient environment: temperature comfortable, music off or low instrumental if preferred
- No phone calls on speaker
- Bottled water offered on entry (single standard)
- No GPS audio playing through cabin speakers — earpiece or silent navigation only
- Smooth driving: no hard braking, no aggressive lane changes
- No unsolicited commentary on traffic or politics

### 4. Drop-Off

- Pull to curb or designated zone — no double-park drop
- Driver exits and opens door
- Luggage unloaded and placed at curb or door
- Sign-off: brief, professional — "Have a great one." — no extended conversation
- 5-star interaction closed: clean, efficient, warm without being intrusive

---

## Service Standards

### Required

- [ ] Vehicle interior: spotless, no odors, no trash
- [ ] Vehicle exterior: washed within 24 hours
- [ ] Driver: business casual or better, groomed, no cologne overload
- [ ] Phone: silent, no personal calls during ride
- [ ] Navigation: pre-loaded, silent, no recalculation drama

### Prohibited

- Eating or drinking during ride (driver)
- Bluetooth audio from driver's personal device
- Asking for tips directly
- Political, religious, or personal opinion conversations unless passenger initiates
- Speeding, hard acceleration, or reckless lane changes

## Driver Profile — The Concierge Standard

The driver is the product. They are not just moving a passenger — they are delivering an experience. The standard is closer to a hotel doorman or business-class flight attendant than a taxi driver.

**Traits:**
- Calm, composed, professional at all times
- Reads the passenger correctly — knows when to talk and when to stay quiet
- Knows LA geography, shortcuts, and event traffic patterns
- Proactive on small details (temperature, route, water) before being asked

**Training:**
- Hospitality fundamentals (silence, presence, service timing)
- Vehicle knowledge (EV range, charging logistics, features)
- Emergency protocols (medical, accident, passenger distress)

## Fleet Operations

### Pre-Shift Checklist (Driver)

- [ ] Exterior wash confirmed (within 24 hrs)
- [ ] Interior vacuumed and wiped — zero odor, zero clutter
- [ ] 2x bottled waters stocked in rear cup holders
- [ ] Charge level: ≥ 80% before first pickup
- [ ] Navigation app pre-loaded with first booking destination
- [ ] Phone on silent, paired to earpiece
- [ ] Attire: business casual minimum — no athletic wear, no strong fragrance
- [ ] Emergency kit present (phone charger for passenger, first aid)

### Charging Logistics (Escalade IQ)

| Scenario | Charge Method | Time to Full |
|---|---|---|
| Overnight base | Level 2 (home/depot) | ~10 hrs |
| Mid-shift top-up | DC fast charge (EA/EVgo) | ~45 min (20→80%) |
| Emergency range | Any L2 commercial | ~2–3 hrs for viable range |

**Rule:** Never start a shift below 60%. Never accept an airport run with less than 40% range unless destination has fast charger confirmed.

### Vehicle Maintenance Schedule

| Item | Interval | Owner |
|---|---|---|

| Exterior wash | Every 24 hrs in operation | Driver |
| Tire inspection | Weekly | Driver |
| Brake / suspension | Per manufacturer schedule | Fleet |
| Software update (OTA) | As released | Fleet |

## Direct Fleet Strategy

The Uber platform is the launch channel — not the destination. The long-term position is direct client relationships that reduce or eliminate Uber's 25% take rate.

### Path to Direct

| Phase | Channel | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| 0–6 months | Uber Black/SUV/Lux | Build rating, volume, repeat riders |
| 6–12 months | Uber + direct referrals | Business cards, QR code, repeat rider outreach |
| 12–24 months | Hybrid | Direct booking (text/app) for regulars; Uber for new acquisition |
| 24+ months | Majority direct | Proprietary dispatch or white-label app |

### Direct Booking Trigger Points

- Rider takes 3+ trips → hand off direct booking option
- Corporate account inquiry → go direct immediately (no Uber cut)
- Event client (premiere, sports, music) → pre-negotiate flat rate

### Target Direct Clients

- Entertainment industry (studio execs, talent, crews)
- Medical / law / finance professionals — LAX regulars
- Hotels (Four Seasons, Waldorf, Bel-Air) — house account arrangements
- Event promoters and venue operators

## Technology Stack

### Current: Uber App (Platform Dependency)

All dispatch, payment, and rating flows through Uber. No data ownership. Zero direct client relationship.

### Near-Term: Driver Ops Layer

Lightweight tooling built and managed in this repo:

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Pre-shift checklist app | Digital checklist enforcement, photo log |
| Trip log / earnings tracker | Per-trip margin calculation |
| Rider CRM (minimal) | Track repeat riders, preferences, direct contact |
| Route intelligence | LA event calendar, surge pattern mapping |

### GenAI Babel Bridge (Differentiator)

Integrated from [WIKI.md](WIKI.md) — the real-time Spanish ↔ English audio translation layer for cross-lingual passenger communication.

**Stack:** AWS (Transcribe → Translate → Polly), WebSocket streaming, sub-200ms latency target.

**Use case:** Driver is comfortable in Spanish; passenger is English-only. Full two-way voice translation in the cabin without phone hand-offs.

**Competitive advantage:** No other LA Black Car operator offers this. Opens the door to serving Spanish-speaking high-net-worth clients and international visitors without a language barrier on either side.
