# LA to Seattle Road Trip

A memorable 1,100+ mile road trip from Los Angeles to Seattle takes 5–8 days, driving north along the Pacific Coast Highway (Highway 1/101) for stunning ocean views, through wine country, redwood forests, and Oregon's dramatic coastline. Top stops include Santa Barbara, Big Sur, Monterey, San Francisco, Redwood National Park, and Cannon Beach.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5]

---

## Key Highlights & Stops

### Days 1–2 — Central Coast


Start with Santa Barbara's beaches and historic mission, then drive the scenic Big Sur coast. Stop at Hearst Castle and spend time at [Monterey Bay Aquarium](https://www.montereybayaquarium.org/) before pushing north.

### Days 3–4 — Northern California


Explore San Francisco's iconic landmarks — Golden Gate Bridge, Fisherman's Wharf, Alcatraz — then head north into the cathedral-like silence of [Redwood National and State Parks](https://www.nps.gov/redw/).[^4]

### Days 5–7 — Oregon Coast & Columbia River Gorge


Experience the dramatic, rocky scenery of the Oregon coast, including Cannon Beach and Haystack Rock. Cut inland to the Columbia River Gorge for waterfall hikes and sweeping river views before crossing into Washington.[^4][^7]

### Day 8 — Arrival in Seattle

Conclude the trip at Seattle Center: Space Needle, Chihuly Garden and Glass, Pike Place Market, and the waterfront.[^1][^2][^6][^7]

## Tips for a Memorable Drive

| Topic | Recommendation |
|-------|----------------|
| **Best Time** | Late spring to early fall — best weather, clearest coastal views |
| **Route** | Highway 101/1 over I-5 for scenery; I-5 if speed matters |
| **Detours** | Yosemite National Park or Crater Lake are worth the extra miles |
| **Pacing** | Don't rush Big Sur — it earns a full day minimum |
| **Lodging** | Book Big Sur and Cannon Beach well in advance (limited options) |
