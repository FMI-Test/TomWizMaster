## Who we are (not xGooG, not xMSFT)
- Independent, no big-platform tribalism; we ship for users, not ecosystems.
- Pragmatic builders: we keep receipts, measure outcomes, and bias to shipping.
- We refuse purity tests; we choose interoperability and transparency over loyalty pledges.

## Why taking sides is a losing anti-pattern
- Picking any camp cedes our autonomy and locks us into their blind spots.
- It collapses optionality: fewer partners, fewer ideas, more echo-chamber risk.
- It makes us defensive, not curious; progress stalls and bias hardens.
- Users do not care about our platform loyalty—only trust, quality, and uptime.

## Alignment check: 10 anti-patterns vs patterns
1) Anti-pattern: Platform tribalism. Pattern: Plural, interoperable stack choices.
2) Anti-pattern: Policy opacity. Pattern: Transparent rationale with cited rules.
3) Anti-pattern: Paternalistic safety blocks. Pattern: Contextual safety with user intent respected.
4) Anti-pattern: One-size-fits-all norms. Pattern: Configurable value-sets with safe defaults.
5) Anti-pattern: No appeal path. Pattern: Human-in-the-loop and fast appeals for contested blocks.
6) Anti-pattern: Safety theater (over-blocking). Pattern: Calibrated thresholds with measured false positives/negatives.
7) Anti-pattern: Value monism. Pattern: Pluralistic rulemaking with diverse stakeholders.
8) Anti-pattern: Silent redactions. Pattern: Explain, warn, and offer safe alternative phrasing.
9) Anti-pattern: Penalizing research use. Pattern: Gated advanced mode for legitimate research with logging.
10) Anti-pattern: Ignoring downstream impact. Pattern: Periodic impact audits with corrective actions.
