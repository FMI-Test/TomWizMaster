# Policy Cost Study Decision Options

## A - Recommended

- Why: Produce a bounded analytical brief with evidence bands, confidence labels, and a clearly separated counterfactual section.
- Dependencies: Public-source cost anchors, explicit caveats, and human review before stronger publication.
- Required updates: Main brief, requirements, change analysis, and traceability matrix.

## B - Simple

- Why: Publish only a short polemical note or manifesto line.
- Trade-offs: Faster, but weak on defensibility and likely to age badly.
- Upgrade path: Expand into Option A by restoring structure, ranges, and source families.

## C - Flexible

- Why: Build a reusable template for future policy-cost studies across other geopolitical chains.
- Costs: More setup, more templates, and more review overhead.
- Dependencies: Reusable schema for conflict episodes, cost classes, and confidence tags.

## Recommendation

Option A is the strongest path because it preserves the user's core question while removing avoidable rhetorical overreach and keeping the result usable as a decision-support brief.
