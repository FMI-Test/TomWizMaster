# Policy Cost Study System Requirements

## Overview

- Scope: A policy-cost brief that estimates the downstream human, economic, and opportunity costs associated with the post-1979 Iranian regional order and surrounding external policy decisions.
- Objectives: Produce a readable, auditable markdown study with explicit confidence labels, scenario boundaries, and counterfactual framing.

## Functional Requirements

- SR-001 - Cover the 1979 Iranian rupture, oil shock effects, Iran-Iraq War, Afghanistan, Iraq, Syria, and post-October 7 costs.
- SR-002 - Distinguish direct costs, indirect costs, and opportunity costs.
- SR-003 - Mark evidence confidence for major numerical claims.
- SR-004 - Separate historical observation from counterfactual inference.
- SR-005 - Include at least one labor-market opportunity-cost calculation.
- SR-006 - Keep the language analytical rather than rhetorical.

## Non-Functional Requirements

- Transparency: Quantitative claims must include scope caveats or provenance notes.
- Neutrality: Avoid single-cause historical certainty when multiple drivers are involved.
- Safety: Exclude operational, tactical, or inciting language.
- Reproducibility: Another reviewer should be able to track each major claim back to a source family or assumption.

## Constraints And Assumptions

- Constraints:
  - Many war-cost estimates vary by date, accounting method, and whether indirect effects are included.
  - Counterfactuals cannot be proven and must be bounded.
  - Some repository source material is rhetorical and requires analytical normalization.
- Assumptions:
  - Public-source ranges are sufficient for magnitude analysis.
  - The study's value is comparative structure, not spurious precision.

## Policies And Governance

- Guardrails: No hate framing, no calls for violence, no dehumanization, and no operational guidance.
- Shared Responsibility: Drafting can summarize evidence, but publication-level claims require human review.
- Escalation And Audit: Any highly contentious claim should be tagged as low-confidence or assumption-dependent.

## Acceptance

- The packet includes a main brief plus options, change analysis, and traceability.
- All major episodes are covered in a structured way.
- The final markdown stays within analytic language and explicit uncertainty bounds.
