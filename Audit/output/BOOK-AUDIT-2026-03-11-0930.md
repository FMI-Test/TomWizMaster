### F3 · P2 · HIGH — Dollar Amount Stripping (Markdown Bold Collision)

- **Severity:** P2
- **Files:** `Kill-Chain-Economics.md`, `Defense-Contractor-Spoiler.md`
- **Pattern:** Dollar figures render as bare `M` and `k` — e.g., `**M**` instead of `**$100M**`
- **Examples:**
  - `Kill-Chain-Economics.md:4` — `Gov pays M → Contractor keeps 98%`
  - `Kill-Chain-Economics.md:7` — `US Gov Cost: **M** (instead of M)`
  - `Defense-Contractor-Spoiler.md:1` — `The Defense Contractor Spoiler: The M Lie`
  - `Defense-Contractor-Spoiler.md:4` — `A contractor employee makes **k**`
- **Root Cause:** Dollar sign (`$`) is interpreted as LaTeX math delimiter in some renderers; amounts may have been stripped during copy-paste or AI-assisted generation
- **Impact:** The economic thesis of the Kill Chain doctrine becomes illegible. For example, the title "The M Lie" is meaningless without `$100M`.
- **Action Required:** Author to restore original dollar amounts and verify against source notes.
