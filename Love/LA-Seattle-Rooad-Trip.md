A memorable 1,100+ mile road trip from Los Angeles to Seattle takes 5–8+ days, driving north along the Pacific Coast Highway (Highway 1/101) for stunning ocean views,, through wine country, redwood forests, and Oregon’s dramatic coastline. Top stops include Santa Barbara, Big Sur, Monterey, San Francisco, Redwood National Park, and Cannon Beach. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]
Key Highlights & Stops

• Central Coast (Days 1-2): Start with Santa Barbara's beaches and mission (https://www.insidethetravellab.com/la-to-santa-barbara-road-trip/), then drive the scenic Big Sur coast, stopping at Hearst Castle and Monterey Bay (https://www.montereybayaquarium.org/).
• Northern California (Days 3-4): Explore San Francisco's landmarks (https://www.travellers-autobarnrv.com/los-angeles-road-trips/los-angeles-to-seattle-road-trip), followed by the towering trees in Redwood National and State Parks (https://www.nps.gov/pore/planyourvisit/lighthouse.htm).
• Oregon Coast & Beyond (Days 5-7): Experience the dramatic, rocky scenery of the Oregon coast, including Cannon Beach (https://www.travellers-autobarnrv.com/los-angeles-road-trips/best-pacific-northwest-road-trip), before heading inland to the Columbia River Gorge.
• Arrival in Seattle: Conclude the trip at the Seattle Center, home to the Space Needle and Chihuly Garden and Glass. [1, 2, 4, 6, 7]

Tips for a Memorable Drive

• Best Time: Late spring to early fall offers the best weather and coastal views.
• Route Choice: While I-5 is faster, taking Highway 101/1 provides a more scenic experience.
• Highlights: Consider adding detours to Yosemite National Park (https://wanderlog.com/drive/between/58145/58154/los-angeles-to-seattle-drive) or Crater Lake. [4, 8, 9, 10, 11]
